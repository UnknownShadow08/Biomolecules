1 Amino Acids
🏠 The Basic Structure ▼
💡Vibe check:

Every amino acid is like an Instagram profile — same layout (α-carbon backbone), but different bio (R group = side chain). That side chain is literally what makes each one unique.

H₂N — CH(R) — COOH
  • α-carbon = central carbon bonded to NH₂, COOH, H, and R
  • 20 standard α-amino acids in living systems
  • All are L-amino acids except Glycine (no optical activity — R = H, so no chiral center)
  • Glycine: NH₂–CH₂–COOH → R group is just H
⚡ Acidic / Basic / Neutral ▼
🧠Think of it like group chats:

Acidic AA = more complainers (COOH) than supporters (NH₂).
Basic AA = more supportive (NH₂) than complaining.
Neutral AA = equal energy, no drama.

⚖️ Zwitterion (Dipolar Ion) ▼
😂No cap analogy:

Imagine you're at peace (neutral charge) but internally chaotic — you're ⊕ on one side and ⊖ on the other. That's a zwitterion. Like someone who looks calm in public but is screaming inside.

H₂N–CH(R)–COOH → –H⁺ → ⁺H₃N–CH(R)–COO⁻ = Zwitterion
  • COOH loses H⁺ → becomes –COO⁻
  • –NH₂ gains that H⁺ → becomes –⁺NH₃
  • Net charge = 0, but has both + and – groups
  • That's why amino acids are amphoteric
📍 Isoelectric Point (pI) ▼
🎯pI = the comfort zone pH:

At pI, the amino acid is in its zwitterion form — chill, not moving anywhere. Make it more acidic (pH < pI) and it gets + charged, runs to –ve plate. Make it basic (pH > pI) and it goes –ve, runs to +ve plate. Classic overreacting to environment energy.

ConditionChargeMoves toward
pH < pIPositive (+ve)Cathode (–ve electrode)
pH = pIZero (neutral)Doesn't move
pH > pINegative (–ve)Anode (+ve electrode)
Neutral AA: pI = (pKa₁ + pKa₂) / 2
Acidic AA: pI = (pKa₁ + pKa₂_sidechain) / 2 → pI < 7
Basic AA: pI = (pKa₂ + pKa₃_sidechain) / 2 → pI > 7
⚠️JEE trap:

Minimum solubility occurs AT the isoelectric point, not at neutral pH. Don't confuse pI with pH = 7.

2 Proteins & Peptides
🔗 Peptide Bond Formation ▼
🤝Like a handshake deal:

–COOH of one AA shakes hands with –NH₂ of another, kicking out H₂O as a byproduct. The resulting bond (–CO–NH–) is the peptide bond. Every protein is just millions of these handshakes in a row.

AA₁–COOH + H₂N–AA₂ → –H₂O → AA₁–CO–NH–AA₂ = Dipeptide
  • Free –NH₂ end = N-terminal (written on LEFT)
  • Free –COOH end = C-terminal (written on RIGHT)
  • Naming: replace –ine with –yl for all except C-terminal AA
  • Gly-Ala-Phe = Glycylalanylphenylalanine
📐Polypeptide count formula:

If X types of amino acids, n molecules → total polypeptides = Xn

3° = complete 3D floor plan
4° = multiple buildings forming a complex (like Haemoglobin with 4 subunits)

🍳 Denaturation — The Glow-Down ▼
😭Main character arc reversed:

Boiling an egg = classic denaturation. The protein was slaying (native form), then heat broke all H-bonds → it unfolds → loses function. It's giving villain origin story.

  • Caused by: heat, extreme pH, chemicals
  • 2° and 3° structure destroyed ✗
  • 1° structure (AA sequence) remains intact ✓
  • Protein loses biological activity
🔗Examples:

Egg white coagulation on boiling · Curdling of milk by bacteria (lacto bacilli)

3 Carbohydrates
🌳 The Family Tree ▼
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦Carbs are a joint family:

Monosaccharides = individuals (can't split further). Disaccharides = couples (2 joined by glycosidic bond). Polysaccharides = joint family of hundreds — all living together, sharing resources (glucose units).

Carbohydrates = polyhydroxy aldehydes or ketones, OR substances that yield these on hydrolysis.

🔴 Reducing vs Non-Reducing Sugar ▼
🔗The logic chain (learn this, not the definition):
Reducing sugar → has FREE hemiacetal group
→ ring can OPEN in solution
→ exposes free –CHO / α-hydroxy C=O
→ can donate electrons to Cu²⁺ / Ag⁺
→ reduces Fehling's / Tollen's / Benedict's ✅
🚨Sucrose — the plot twist:

Sucrose is a disaccharide but NON-REDUCING. Why? Because BOTH anomeric carbons (C1 of glucose AND C2 of fructose) are locked in the glycosidic bond. Both hands tied. Can't open ring. Can't reduce. It's the prisoner of disaccharides.

🍇 Glucose — The GOAT Monosaccharide ▼
🏆Glucose is that main character:

Known as dextrose (rotates polarized light to right = dextrorotatory). Found everywhere: grapes, honey, blood. Both α and β forms exist — they interconvert in water through mutarotation. Can't pick a lane.

  • Molecular formula: C₆H₁₂O₆ (aldohexose)
  • D-Glucose: OH on C5 is on the RIGHT in Fischer projection
  • α-D-Glucose: OH on C1 is DOWN (axial in Haworth) — specific rotation +112°
  • β-D-Glucose: OH on C1 is UP (equatorial in Haworth) — specific rotation +19°
  • β form is more stable (equatorial OH = less steric clash)
6-membered ring = Pyranose (like Pyran)
5-membered ring = Furanose (like Furan)
🔄 Mutarotation — The Flip ▼
🔄Can't make up its mind:

Dissolve pure α-glucose (rotation +112°) in water. It slowly shifts to +52.7° — an equilibrium mixture. Pure β-glucose (rotation +19°) also shifts to +52.7° from below. Both vibe at +52.7° = mutarotation. The open-chain form is the middle-man in this process.

α (+112°) ⇌ open chain ⇌ β (+19°) → equilibrium at +52.7°
📌Rule:

Only REDUCING sugars mutarotate (need free hemiacetal group to open). Sucrose? No mutarotation. Glycosides? No mutarotation.

⚗️ Glucose Reactions — The Cheat Sheet ▼
🔗Why does glucose fail Schiff's test if it has –CHO?

Because only 0.02% of glucose exists in open-chain form. The cyclic forms (α/β) dominate. The –CHO is "hiding" inside the ring. Weak tests like Schiff's can't detect that tiny 0.02%. But strong oxidising agents like Tollen's/Fehling's can.

⚠️The fructose trap:

Fructose is a KETOSE but PASSES Tollen's/Fehling's. In basic medium, fructose ⇌ enediol ⇌ glucose/mannose (tautomerism). So it can reduce Cu²⁺. But Br₂/H₂O (neutral, mild) = fructose FAILS. Use Br₂/H₂O to tell aldose from ketose.

🧠Linkage hack:

🔀Inversion of sucrose explained:

Sucrose is dextrorotatory (+66.5°). After hydrolysis: glucose (+52.7°) + fructose (–92.2°). Fructose has a bigger rotation magnitude. The mixture goes LAEVOROTATORY. Rotation literally inverts sign. That's why it's called "inversion sugar." Main character moment for fructose.

🍓 Fructose — The Sweet Rival ▼
🍭Fructose is the sweeter sibling:

Fructose = sweetest monosaccharide. It's a ketohexose (C=O at C2 not C1). Forms 5-membered FURANOSE ring (C2 to C5 closure). While glucose likes 6-membered rings, fructose is the rebel doing 5-membered.

  • Specific rotation: –92° (laevorotatory = D-fructose but laevo)
  • α-D-fructose: rotation –21°; β-D-fructose: –133°
  • Forms fructofuranose (5-membered ring)
  • Reduction → Sorbitol + Mannitol (two products because C2 is new asymmetric center)
4 Nucleic Acids
🧱 Building Blocks Hierarchy ▼
🏗️Like LEGO sets:

Nucleic acid = assembled model. Nucleotide = one LEGO brick WITH the stud (phosphate). Nucleoside = brick WITHOUT the stud. Sugar + nitrogenous base = the plastic part. Phosphate = the stud that connects them.

Nitrogenous Base + Pentose Sugar = Nucleoside + Phosphate = Nucleotide → many → Nucleic Acid
  • DNA sugar = Deoxyribose (no OH at C2)
  • RNA sugar = Ribose (OH at C2)
  • Phosphodiester bond: between 5' carbon of one nucleotide and 3' carbon of next
💍 Bases — Singles vs Couples ▼
🧠Never forget this:

"PURE As Gold" → Purines = Adenine + Guanine (double ring)
"CUT the Py" → Pyrimidines = Cytosine + Uracil + Thymine (single ring)

🔗GC = 3 H-bonds = stronger:

Like a triple knot vs a double knot. More GC pairs in DNA → higher melting temperature. This is a classic MCQ trap.

📏 Chargaff's Rules ▼
  • [A] = [T] always; [G] = [C] always in double-stranded DNA
  • Purines = Pyrimidines in total quantity
  • A/T ratio ≈ 1; G/C ratio ≈ 1
  • A+T/G+C ratio = constant for a species
  • One strand: 5'-CAT TAG GAC-3' → other strand: 3'-GTA ATC CTG-5'
🔗Implication:

If you know one strand's sequence, you automatically know the other. The two strands are COMPLEMENTARY and ANTIPARALLEL. They run in opposite 5'→3' directions.

5 Vitamins
💧 Fat vs Water Soluble ▼
🔥The ultimate mnemonic:

FAT = ADEK
Vitamins A, D, E, K are Fat-soluble. Stored in liver and adipose tissue. Don't need daily intake.

WATER = B + C
B-complex and C are water-soluble. Excreted in urine. Need daily supply.

6 Enzymes
⚙️ What Are Enzymes? ▼
🤖Enzymes = biological robots:

Each one does ONE specific job — can't do anything else. Like a vending machine that only gives one snack. Insanely efficient (1 enzyme can process ~1 million molecules/min). But very fussy about temperature and pH — wrong conditions and they ghost you (denaturation).

  • Protein molecules of high molecular mass
  • Form colloidal solutions in water
  • First enzyme synthesized in lab = 1969
  • Optimum temperature: 298–310 K (body temp = 310 K)
  • Optimum pH: 5–7 (pepsin works at pH ~2)
